Design Principles

Hicks Law

Technoblade

The principal of Hicks law is that the moment a user lands on a website they should be able to see what they are on the website for. Technoblade is a popular MineCraft Youtuber, the entire purpose of his website is to sell his merchandise. When you first land on the website in mobile view immediatly it shows the most recent merchandise that is offered on the site. This is the most likely thing that people would be on the website for and will give users the immediate option of selecting it. In the case that this is not what they are here for they will be able to quickly and easily scroll down for other options of new arrivals, or select the menu bar to choose something else. By limiting the options available they have shown exactly what the users are going to be interested in seeing.

Fitt's Law

Critical Role

Fitts law states that once landing on the page all relevent information should be accessible with minimal movement. Critical Role does a great job of making sure all the information is immediatly within reach. The use of the carousel to give more information within immediate reach. The most important thing though is that the "latest" episode button is right in the lower middle of the screen. The user can reach that button even if their hand is on the lower part of the phone or holding it from the back.

Negative Space

Not Another Dungeons and Dragons Podcast

The negative space here really stands out to emphasize the most important information available. This specific section is an introduction to "The Direct Messenger." The negative space is used as a lighter grey color, which has some simple shapes in side of it. Then the bright and dark colors contrast, which makes them stand out even more distinctly. I especially like the use of the green to emphasize the "Don't @ me!" quote, it stands out so much against the negative space.
